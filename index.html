To Space and Back

A fraction learning space / rocket game

To Space and Back is a fraction learning game for 3rd-5th graders. The game is designed to introduce the concepts of fractions through a graphical representation of fractions as slices of circles and rectangles. The game is made to accompany the initial steps in the fractions learning trajectory for the Common Core curriculum for our target age group.

This project was made with an emphasis on accessibility for students of various backgrounds through the implementation of customization and references. Players can choose between different background music options, text-to-speech, and various languages. We also took into account users with various disabilities, including ESL, auditory impairments, visual impairments, attention deficit. This game was made in collaboration with Zack Crenshaw and is made/run using Java.

Friendship Quest

A 2D RPG fantasy friendship simulator

Dreamdrift: Friendship Quest is an action/adventure RPG about finding friendships within a capitalist world. This demo explores the dialogue and choices throughout part of our main storyline and reveals the backgrounds of key characters. The player’s objective is to travel toward the “Dreamdrift Isles”, an unreachable fantasy land that represents the anxiety and uncertainty shared by our generation in the modern world.

Choice is the main mechanic in the game. Throughout the game, players encounter NPCs whose dialogue encourages them to make choices within the gameworld. These choices affect the gameplay through a branching narrative. Players also encounter battles, a gold system, and cutscenes. This project was made in a group of 5 using RPG Maker MV, and is available for Mac and Windows.

Situational Awareness

Web app prototype based on research for the hearing impaired

This project focuses on how hearing impaired users navigate the UChicago campus. Specifically, we were interested in learning the form and under what circumstances hearing impaired students might benefit from technology which improves situational awareness.

Our group of 3 conducted research, semi-structured interviews, and user evalutations to create low and high-fidelity prototypes. In our final iteration, we made a wearable device that detects audio input and relays it to the customizable web app. This app then determines what the sound was and alerts the user through vibration and visual cues such as a sound log and real-time popup. This project was made using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.

Haptic Camera

A haptic handheld phone-camera for eyes-free interactions

The purpose of this project was to create a phone case that allows one to take photos without needing to see the screen. It is designed to give haptic feedback, with proper ergonomics and intuitve enough to use eyes-free. We conducted user evaluations and made multiple iterations, both modeled and 3D printed.

Our final prototype is a slim fitting phone case (fit to iPhone 8+) that features a back dial to switch between 4 camera modes, a zoom slider, and toggle switches for turning on flash and videomode. Additionally, photos are taken with a spring-like button on the underside of the phone when it is held in landscape. These prototypes were made using Tinkercad and Flashprint.

Forex Bots

Forex trading bots results and twitter bot currency wordcloud generator

This project uses algorithmic bot generation in order to trade currencies in the forex market. I used forexrobotacademy.com to generate forex trading bots for the top 5 major currency pairs, and iteratively generated and retired bots based on performances.

On the website, one can view the updated results in the form of a balance chart over time. The user can also click a button to view the detailed statement for the account including all trades, profit, and various other results.

In addition to the results, the user has the ability to use the buttons on the home screen to generate a Twitter wordcloud based on a currency being traded, and see the top words / sentiment for that currency in the 50 most recent tweets. This project was made using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript (Express/Node.js) and is deployed on Heroku.

Breach Notification

The Effect of Breach Notifications on Victims of Previous Data Breaches

In light of the increasing number of data breaches and their toll on individuals’ privacy and security, it is important to design breach notifications that equip affected individuals with the knowledge they need to secure their data in the aftermath of a breach. This paper presents results from a pilot study that investigated the effect of differences in breach notification design on the actions that affected users think they should take take, as well as on their understanding of the breach.

As part of our study, we created two breach notifications – one with the accounts involved in breaches and specific details on the types of data at risk, and one with just the accounts involved in breaches. We then sent the notifications to participants, and we later interviewed the participants about the actions they took or were planning to take and about their perception of the notification and general information about their perception of data breaches.

EU Online Privacy

Effect of European Union on concerns about online privacy.

In this research, I investigated how Europeans’ feelings about the European Union, under which they are regulated, affect their concerns about online privacy and personal information. I hypothesized that those who have higher levels of satisfaction with their nation's union will be less concerned with privacy risks involved with sharing personal information online as well as the amount of control they have over this information.

The dataset that I used was by the European Commission in 2015 through survey questionnaires, by the means of in-person interviews, proportionally sampled to represent the population of the 28 countries in the European Union at the time. Through using survey responses by the means of an ordinal feeling thermometer / scale toward the European Union and an ordinal concern thermometer toward sharing personal information online, I could directly measure the theory in an empirical manner. In my regression, I control for each participant’s trust in their government and country, their internet usage, age, gender, education, marital status, political affiliation, and satisfaction with life. Data analysis was done using R.

Search Sentiment

Sentiment analyzer addon for AI generated Google searches

This project is addressing the bias, lack of representation, and misrepresentation of minorities (specifically black people and culture) in AI. These issues range from healthcare, prison systems, image matching, speech, and more popularly facial recognition. This project focuses on AI search result generation bias in the form of a Firefox addon. The goal of this tool is to understand and reveal the hidden impact of AI and what type of sentiment (analyzed numerically) that it produces when being used. Its focus is on Google Search and the results / sentiment scores that it gives for various inputs.

This Firefox addon uses the user's browser to make Google Search queries with different classifiers (ie "black" and "white") appended to the query. It then analyzes each search result in terms of sentiment score (how negative or positive the text is). This addon was made in JavaScript using HTML and CSS elements. Example usage, roadmaps, future plans, and the download can all be found on the GitHub repository with more details in the README.

Takuzu

Takuzu (Binary Sudoku) with random 4x4 and 6x6 games

The goal of this project was to implement a playable number puzzle called Takazu (also known as Binary Sudoku). It involves the placement of two symbols, often 1s and 0s, on a square grid. The objective is to fill the grid with 1s and 0s, where there is an equal number of 1s and 0s in each row and column and no more than two of either number adjacent to each other. Additionally, there can be no identical rows or columns. In this implementation, 1s and 0s are represented by squares of colors blue and red.

This game is in the form of a web page and has the following features: Click based interactions, compatability with different screen sizes, solution/validity checker, pre-generated and random-generated game boards. It was built and compiled using Elm. A full report can be found on the GitHub repo and the game can be played at the website linked.
